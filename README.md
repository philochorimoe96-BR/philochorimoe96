Project Proposal: Tech for Trust

·         Project Title: Tech for Trust: Youth-Led Digital Peacebuilding and Social Cohesion in Kakuma and Kalobeyei

·         Target Location: Kakuma Refugee Camp (1–4), Kalobeyei Integrated Settlement, and Kakuma Town Host Communities (Turkana West, Kenya)

·         Implementing Organization: Amani Grassroots Network

·         Project Duration: 12 Months

·         Requested Budget: $40,000 (Approx. KES 5,200,000)

1. Executive Summary

The proposed project aims to combat rising online polarization, targeted misinformation, and ethnic hate speech circulating on social media platforms within the Kakuma and Kalobeyei ecosystems. Over 12 months, the initiative will build the capacity of 40 young refugee and host-community content creators as "Digital Peacebuilders". By deploying community-led social media campaigns, running digital literacy bootcamps, and utilizing localization strategies to counter online triggers, this project directly prevents digital hostilities from escalating into real-world, physical inter-communal conflict.

2. Problem Statement & Context Analysis

A quiet digital shift is unfolding across Turkana West. With expanded local solar-powered Wi-Fi networks and growing smartphone access, thousands of youth across Kakuma and Kalobeyei utilize platforms like TikTok, Facebook, and WhatsApp daily. However, this rapid digitization presents unique peacebuilding vulnerabilities:

·         The Conflict: Deepening humanitarian aid cuts and environmental pressures create friction between nationalities and the host community. This tension frequently bleeds into digital spaces as targeted cyberbullying, identity-based hostility, and viral rumors. The Root Causes: A lack of algorithmic literacy, digital desperation among unemployed youth, and coordinated disinformation weaponized to turn diverse communities against one another.]

·         The Impact: Distrust actively fragments the camp's social fabric. Minor online slurs quickly ripple offline, threatening the safety of shared community markets, football games, and water collection points.

 

3. Project Objectives

·         Objective 1: Identify, train, and mentor 40 digital influencers (20 refugees from diverse blocks and 20 host community youth) in fact-checking, peace narratives, and digital safety by Month 3.

·         Objective 2: Establish an open-source, hybrid offline-online community listening and rapid-response mechanism to track and report toxic local trends weekly.

·         Objective 3: Reach at least 50,000 platform users across Kakuma and Kalobeyei with multi-lingual, short-form visual content promoting unity, inclusion, and peaceful coexistence.

4. Theory of Change

IF influential refugee and host-community youth are equipped with advanced digital peacebuilding skills, and IF they produce hyper-local counter-narratives that debunk viral rumors, THEN online polarization will decrease, BECAUSE the digital ecosystem will be anchored by local voices driving social cohesion, making manipulation ineffective.

5. Project Activities & Implementation Plan

Phase 1: Context Mapping & Stakeholder Alignment (Months 1–3)

·         Digital Sentiment Assessments: Use social listening tools (such as the open-source platform The "Umati" Framework (Ushahidi) to trace local keywords, hashtags, and memes inciting friction.

·         Participant Selection: Recruit tech-active youth leaders, local TikTok creators, and community-led organization (CBO) volunteers across Kakuma 1-4 and Kalobeyei.

Phase 2: Capacity Building & Content Factory (Months 4–8)

·         Digital Peacebuilding Bootcamps: Deliver 4-day intensive training blocks covering non-violent communication, deep-fake spot-checking, and digital security.

·         Localization Lab: Produce high-impact, short-form videos and audio clips translated into local languages (Turkana, Somali, Arabic, Dinka, Swahili, etc.) targeting humanitarian resource rumors.

Phase 3: Mitigation & Offline Integration (Months 9–12)

·         Digital Safety Peer Networks: Form rapid-response WhatsApp and Telegram desks where youth quickly flag and verify misleading local media.

·         Hybrid Dialogue Fora: Host live, offline "TikTok for Peace" community showcases alongside local partner centers (like the DRC Kakuma Youth Centre) to tie virtual momentum directly to physical community elders and leaders.

6. Target Beneficiaries

·         Direct Beneficiaries: 40 youth content creators trained; 20 community peace committee elders paired for validation.

·         Indirect Beneficiaries: Over 50,000 active internet-using residents across the 300,000+ population of Kakuma and Kalobeyei who will interact with safer online content.
